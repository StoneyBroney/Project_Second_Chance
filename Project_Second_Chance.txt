<header>
<h1>Project Second Chancel</h1>
<p><em>Reducing waste, empowering communities</em></p>
</header>

<section>
<h2>Mission Statement</h2>
<p>To reduce waste and empower communities by collecting gently used clothing, electronics, and furniture, then redistributing them for free or at low cost to families in need.</p>
</section>

<section>
<h2>Core Goals</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Environmental Impact</strong> – keep usable goods out of landfills.</li>
<li><strong>Community Support</strong> – provide essentials to low-income households.</li>
<li><strong>Skill-Building</strong> – offer repair workshops (fixing clothes, electronics, bikes, etc.) so people can learn practical skills.</li>
<li><strong>Youth Involvement</strong> – create volunteer opportunities for teens and young adults, encouraging leadership and community service.</li>
</ul>
</section>

<section>
<h2>How It Works</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Donations:</strong> Partner with schools, local businesses, and residents to collect used but functional items.</li>
<li><strong>Distribution:</strong> Open a “community hub” where families can pick up what they need.</li>
<li><strong>Workshops:</strong> Host weekly events where volunteers teach basic repair, sewing, and recycling skills.</li>
<li><strong>Eco-Education:</strong> Run awareness campaigns about sustainability and reducing consumer waste.</li>
</ul>
</section>

<section>
<h2>Funding Sources</h2>
<ul>
<li>Small grants from local government and foundations.</li>
<li>Corporate sponsorships (e.g., stores donating excess inventory).</li>
<li>Fundraisers (community yard sales, eco-fairs, donation drives).</li>
</ul>
</section>

<section>
<h2>Impacts</h2>
<ul>
<li>Helps families save money.</li>
<li>Strengthens community bonds.</li>
<li>Promotes sustainability.</li>
<li>Creates volunteer and leadership opportunities.</li>
</ul>
</section>
